# Startup Funding Analysis in India (2015–2024)

## Tools Used
- SQL
- Python
- Power BI


## Key Business Insights

1. Startup funding in India is highly concentrated in metro cities, with Bengaluru alone accounting for the largest share of total funding.

2. E-Commerce, FinTech, and Consumer Internet sectors dominate overall funding, indicating strong investor confidence in scalable digital business models.

3. Seed and Series A stages record the highest number of deals, highlighting strong early-stage startup activity.

4. A small group of institutional investors contributes a disproportionate share of total funding, indicating high capital concentration.

5. Funding activity peaked around 2015–2018 and declined significantly post-2020, reflecting market corrections and macroeconomic impacts.


## Business Recommendations

1. Investors may explore emerging cities beyond metro hubs to capture early-stage growth opportunities at lower valuations.

2. Startups in non-dominant sectors should focus on differentiation and niche innovation to attract funding.

3. Policymakers can promote startup growth by improving access to funding in Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities.

4. Early-stage founders should prioritize seed and Series A readiness, as these stages attract the most investor attention.
